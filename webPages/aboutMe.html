My Experience

I graduated from the University of Connecticut in 2020 with a Bachelor's of Science in Geoscience. During my time there, I worked in a lab with my professor as a GIS (Geographic Information Systems) Specialist, using software to map stonewalls all around New England. This experience and the research that came with it helped me land a role during my senior year as the GIS Specialist for the UCONN IT Department. During my time there, I learned a lot about the IT environment and how professional tech work is carried out. I enjoyed working on my senior capstone project, and thanks to coaching from my amazing professor and grad student mentor I won the UCONN Department of Geosciences 2020 Undergrad Research Award. During my final semester I absolutely knew that I wanted to get a job in GIS working with computers.

My class of 2020 graduated to a world in chaos, and, like many of my classmates, I took the first job I could get out of college. Since then, I've had experiences working in fields that gave me exposure to different walks of life. I've worked as a Construction Inspector, a Construction Superintendent Trainee, and my latest job has been as an Administrative Coordinator at a ski area in Vermont.

I wanted to get back to my GIS roots. Even though I had plenty of experience in college, I was having trouble landing a job in that field because of my lack of coding skills, which any full Computer Science or GIS major would have. So, I enrolled in an online coding platform, Scrimba, and took courses on Python, the most prominent language used for GIS software. Then, I became hooked on a new course offering, the Frontend Developer Career Path course.

At the same time, I learned basic Wordpress skills as my job at the ski area. I updated information on the website, such as descriptions for certain pages, created ads for local businesses, and created new web pages. The more I learned about Wordpress the more fascinating it became. The combination of working with Wordpress and taking Scrimba's Frontend Developer course made me realize I really wanted to get into Web Design and development. I am currently learning the web application framework Ruby on Rails and would like to be a fullstack developer one day.

On a more personal note, I currently live in the beautiful state of Vermont. I have started taken up photography as a hobby; every image on this website was taken by yours truly. Check out my Photography section for more!
